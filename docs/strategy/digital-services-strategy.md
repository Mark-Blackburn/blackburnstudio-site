# Digital Services Strategy

Status: Provisional

Last reviewed: 2026-06-28

## Purpose

This document defines a provisional initial service model for Digital within Blackburn Studio.

It should be read alongside [brand-architecture.md](brand-architecture.md), [target-clients.md](target-clients.md), and the current-state documentation in [../current-state/site-overview.md](../current-state/site-overview.md) and [../current-state/brand-baseline.md](../current-state/brand-baseline.md).

## Positioning summary

Approved working direction:

- Digital should have an accessible entry point built around professional websites.
- Deeper capability should remain visible for clients who need more than a standard brochure site.
- The language should remain practical and restrained.
- Technology should follow understanding of the underlying business problem.

Approved working direction for launch planning:

- Websites are the primary Digital entry-point offer.
- Workflow and process improvement is a visible core Digital capability.
- Platforms, portals and business systems are possible implementation outcomes where appropriate.
- Ongoing digital improvement is a secondary or follow-on offer rather than a lead message.
- Digital pricing is not published at launch.

This service model is not yet a final public offer, package structure, or pricing model.

## Service groups

Approved working direction hierarchy:

1. Websites
2. Workflow and process improvement
3. Platforms, portals and business systems
4. Ongoing digital improvement

### Websites

Possible scope:

- business websites
- portfolio websites
- service-business websites
- website redesigns
- responsive implementation
- accessibility-conscious development
- search and metadata foundations
- deployment and domain configuration
- photography and content integration

Client problem addressed:

- the client needs a professional, usable website that reflects the business well and is manageable in practice

Example deliverables:

- small business brochure website
- portfolio site
- service-led site with enquiry pathways
- redesigned site replacing an outdated or ineffective current presence

Likely client type:

- small businesses
- independent professionals
- service providers
- community organisations
- creative practices

Boundaries and exclusions:

- not a claim to handle broad marketing operations
- not a promise of enterprise-scale platform delivery within this service group
- not a final commitment to fixed packages or price tiers

Evidence or case studies eventually needed:

- before-and-after website examples
- examples of improved clarity, usability, or maintainability
- examples where photography and website delivery worked well together

Website prominence recommendation:

- primary service

### Workflow and process improvement

Preferred public-facing service label:

- Workflow and process improvement

Supporting terminology (as needed):

- process modernisation
- paper-to-digital workflows
- operational improvement
- digital workflow design
- practical automation
- business process discovery

Language to avoid as lead positioning:

- digital transformation
- management consulting
- enterprise transformation
- innovation consultancy

Possible scope:

- reviewing current workflows
- process mapping
- identifying bottlenecks and repeated work
- reducing duplicated data entry
- reviewing paper forms and manual records
- moving appropriate processes from paper to digital
- improving spreadsheet and email-driven processes
- identifying missed opportunities for self-service
- finding improvements the business owner may not have considered
- prioritising improvements by value, effort and operational risk
- preparing a practical improvement roadmap
- implementing small improvements before larger systems where appropriate

Client problem addressed:

- the client knows that part of the business is slow, frustrating, fragile, or dependent on manual knowledge, but may not know the real cause or the right digital solution

Example deliverables:

- workflow review
- current-state process map
- prioritised improvement recommendations
- paper-to-digital process design
- simplified forms and approvals
- operational improvement roadmap
- proof of concept
- staged implementation plan
- implemented digital workflow

Likely client type:

- organisations with manual operational friction
- businesses relying on paper, spreadsheets, and long email chains
- owners who can see inefficiency but cannot yet define the right solution

Boundaries and exclusions:

- not generic management consulting
- not large enterprise transformation programmes
- not technology implementation without understanding the process
- not automating a poor process without first considering simplification
- not making unsupported financial or productivity claims

Evidence or case studies eventually needed:

- examples of clarified workflow ownership
- examples of reduced duplication or simplified handoffs
- evidence for published outcome claims where permission exists

Website prominence recommendation:

- visible core Digital capability
- secondary to Websites as the simplest entry point, but more prominent than an implementation-only automation subsection

### Platforms, portals and business systems

Possible scope:

- client portals
- member portals
- administration systems
- dashboards
- content and data-driven applications
- custom workflows that exceed a standard website
- workflow implementation after discovery and process simplification
- practical automation where it is the appropriate outcome

Client problem addressed:

- the client needs a structured digital implementation after workflow review, with an access model or operational flow that cannot be served well by a standard website alone

Example deliverables:

- member area for an organisation
- client-facing portal
- internal administration dashboard
- custom application for structured content or process handling
- staged rollout of workflow improvements with portal or system components

Likely client type:

- clubs and member organisations
- service businesses with client process needs
- organisations with repeatable but awkward manual workflows

Boundaries and exclusions:

- not positioned as mass custom-software outsourcing
- not a claim of large-agency staffing capacity
- not a promise that every request fits a portal or platform model
- not a starting assumption before discovery work has clarified the underlying need

Evidence or case studies eventually needed:

- examples of workflow improvement
- examples of controlled access, administration, or reporting value
- evidence of reliable delivery for more complex builds

Website prominence recommendation:

- secondary service, but visible as deeper capability

### Ongoing digital improvement

Possible scope:

- website maintenance
- content updates
- technical improvements
- hosting and deployment support
- performance and accessibility reviews
- phased feature development

Client problem addressed:

- the client needs continuity after launch and does not want the website or digital system to become neglected or difficult to maintain

Example deliverables:

- maintenance arrangement
- periodic improvements
- content support
- technical review and prioritised fixes

Likely client type:

- website clients needing continuity
- organisations without internal technical ownership
- clients with phased or evolving needs

Boundaries and exclusions:

- not yet a final retainer model
- not a commitment to 24/7 support expectations
- not a claim to act as an outsourced IT department

Evidence or case studies eventually needed:

- examples of sustained improvement over time
- evidence of responsive support and stable upkeep
- examples of phased delivery after initial launch

Website prominence recommendation:

- secondary service

## Service hierarchy recommendation

Approved working direction:

- present Websites as the clearest entry-point service
- present Workflow and process improvement as the lead deeper capability
- present Platforms, portals and business systems as implementation pathways where appropriate
- treat automation as one possible outcome of workflow discovery, not the starting assumption
- present Ongoing digital improvement as a follow-on service rather than the lead message

## Services not currently proposed

Approved working direction exclusions for launch:

- general computer repair
- consumer technical support
- broad social-media management
- paid advertising management
- branding-only engagements
- commodity hosting resale
- pretending to provide large-agency staffing capacity

## Strategic assumptions introduced here

- Blackburn Studio should enter Digital with a credible website offer first.
- Workflow and process understanding should precede major technology choices.
- More complex capability should remain visible, but not dominate the entry message.
- The practice should avoid large-agency breadth claims.
