# Digital Discovery Commercial Model

Status: Provisional

Last reviewed: 2026-06-28

## Purpose

Define the provisional commercial and delivery model for discovery work within Blackburn Studio Digital.

## Why discovery has commercial value

- Discovery reduces implementation risk by clarifying the real problem before solution commitment.
- Discovery can identify practical improvements not initially requested by the client.
- Discovery helps avoid paying to automate inefficient or unnecessary process steps.
- Discovery outputs can be actionable even where no implementation follows.

## Included versus standalone discovery

### Included project discovery

Included discovery is part of defined website or digital implementation projects and is proportionate to project scope.

Included project discovery should cover enough to understand:

- business goals
- intended users
- relevant stakeholders
- the current workflow
- content and information needs
- constraints
- obvious process issues affecting the proposed solution
- useful improvements that should influence project scope

Included discovery does not include unlimited or organisation-wide process review.

### Paid standalone discovery

Standalone paid discovery is appropriate when:

- the client knows there is a problem but cannot define it clearly
- several processes, people, or systems are involved
- the correct solution is not yet known
- significant paper-to-digital change is being considered
- multiple implementation stages may be required
- the client wants independent recommendation before committing to implementation
- the most appropriate recommendation may not involve custom software

Standalone discovery should deliver useful findings even where implementation does not proceed.

## Provisional discovery levels

These are internal service-design levels and not final public packages.

### 1) Project discovery

Typical use:

- defined website
- small digital workflow
- bounded implementation project

Possible activities:

- owner or stakeholder discussion
- goals and audience review
- current-state summary
- content and functional requirements
- constraints
- high-level opportunity identification
- scope confirmation

Possible outputs:

- agreed problem statement
- project brief
- recommended scope
- assumptions and exclusions
- implementation direction
- proposal inputs

### 2) Workflow review

Typical use:

- one identifiable process
- paper-based workflow
- repeated spreadsheet or email administration
- approval or handoff problem
- self-service opportunity

Possible activities:

- stakeholder interviews
- current-state process mapping
- friction and risk identification
- duplicated data review
- manual handling review
- future-state process design
- technology-option assessment
- prioritisation

Possible outputs:

- current-state workflow
- identified friction and risks
- recommended future process
- improvement opportunities
- prioritised recommendations
- implementation options
- staged next steps

### 3) Digital improvement discovery

Typical use:

- several connected processes
- unclear or broad operational needs
- organisation-level paper-to-digital opportunities
- phased platform or system work

Possible activities:

- broader stakeholder consultation
- workflow and system inventory
- opportunity mapping
- dependency identification
- risk and readiness assessment
- prioritisation by value, effort and operational impact
- phased planning

Possible outputs:

- discovery findings
- opportunity map
- prioritised improvement portfolio
- recommended sequencing
- quick wins
- longer-term initiatives
- proposed implementation phases
- evidence and measurement recommendations

## Qualification criteria

- level of uncertainty about root problem
- number of stakeholders or operational handoffs
- process complexity and risk
- urgency and operational impact
- confidence in implementation direction
- suitability for staged delivery

## Activities

- interviews and stakeholder consultation
- process and workflow mapping
- friction and risk analysis
- opportunity identification and prioritisation
- future-state options definition
- implementation planning at proportionate depth

## Deliverables

- discovery summary
- current-state and future-state workflow views
- prioritised recommendations
- implementation options and sequencing
- assumptions, exclusions, and dependencies
- evidence and measurement recommendations where relevant

## Scope boundaries

- discovery is bounded by agreed scope, depth, and stakeholder access
- discovery is not indefinite advisory support
- discovery is not large enterprise transformation consulting
- discovery does not guarantee a particular implementation method

## Stakeholder assumptions

- sponsor access is available for key decisions
- relevant process owners participate in discovery
- current tools, forms, and workflow artifacts are available for review
- constraints and non-negotiables are disclosed early

## Client responsibilities

- nominate decision owner and key stakeholders
- provide timely access to process information
- review findings and confirm priorities
- validate assumptions and constraints
- support implementation sequencing decisions

## Change-control considerations

- scope expansion beyond agreed discovery depth should trigger review
- additional stakeholder groups or process areas may require revised effort
- material objective changes should be documented and re-aligned
- implementation estimates should be revisited when discovery findings materially change scope

## When discovery can move directly into implementation

- problem definition is clear enough for scoped delivery
- priority actions are agreed and feasible
- stakeholder alignment is sufficient for execution
- risks are understood at an acceptable level

## When a separate proposal is required

- discovery reveals broader or different scope than expected
- multiple implementation paths need commercial comparison
- phased programme structure is needed
- delivery approach differs materially from initial assumptions

## When Blackburn Studio may recommend no technology change

- process simplification alone resolves the major friction
- policy, ownership, or sequencing changes are higher value than new tooling
- operational readiness is insufficient for responsible implementation
- expected benefit does not justify implementation complexity or risk

## Intellectual-property and deliverable considerations requiring later legal review

- ownership and reuse rights for discovery artifacts
- client rights to use recommendations with third-party implementers
- attribution limits for adapted or partial implementations
- treatment of templates, methods, and internal frameworks
- confidentiality and publication permissions for discovery outputs

## Unresolved pricing questions

- discovery pricing method by level and scope
- minimum engagement size and included effort
- threshold where included discovery moves to separate paid discovery
- treatment of phased discovery billing and change requests

## Unresolved contracting questions

- proposal and contract structure for included versus standalone discovery
- acceptance criteria for discovery deliverables
- change-control triggers and approval handling
- dependencies and assumption handling in contracts

## Capacity and scheduling considerations

- availability for interviews and review cycles
- time required for synthesis between workshops/interviews
- project sequencing when discovery and implementation overlap
- calendar buffering for stakeholder-response delays

## Evidence required before publishing claims

- documented evidence source for each claimed outcome
- permission status for named or anonymised publication
- clear attribution of Blackburn Studio contribution
- verification that claims remain proportionate and accurate
