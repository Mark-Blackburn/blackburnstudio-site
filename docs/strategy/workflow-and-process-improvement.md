# Workflow and Process Improvement

Status: Provisional

Last reviewed: 2026-06-28

## Purpose

Define how Blackburn Studio Digital approaches workflow review, process simplification, and staged implementation before selecting technology.

Core strategy principle:

> Do not digitise waste. Understand and simplify the process before automating it.

This principle is internal strategy language and is not automatically final website copy.

## Client situations this service addresses

- Work still runs through paper forms, manual records, or ad-hoc documents.
- Teams repeatedly re-key the same information.
- Approvals and follow-ups are managed through long email chains.
- Spreadsheets are acting as informal operational systems.
- Key operational knowledge is concentrated in one person.
- Owners know something is inefficient but cannot define the right solution.
- Staff and customers rely on manual handoffs where self-service may be possible.

## Discovery approach

- Understand the real business context before prescribing technology.
- Map how work currently moves through people, tools, and decisions.
- Identify friction, duplication, delay, and fragile dependencies.
- Distinguish symptoms from root causes.
- Prioritise improvements by value, implementation effort, and operational risk.
- Stage implementation so change is manageable.

## Discovery delivery model

Approved working direction:

- Discovery is available in both forms:
	- included project discovery
	- paid standalone discovery

Included project discovery applies when:

- the client has a defined website or bounded digital implementation project
- enough discovery is needed to understand goals, users, stakeholders, workflow, constraints, and obvious process issues affecting scope

Included project discovery boundaries:

- proportionate to the implementation project
- not an unlimited or organisation-wide process review

Paid standalone discovery applies when:

- the client knows there is a problem but cannot define it clearly
- several processes, people, or systems are involved
- the correct solution is not yet known
- significant paper-to-digital change is being considered
- independent recommendations are needed before implementation commitment

How discovery leads into implementation:

- discovery findings inform whether implementation should proceed immediately, in stages, or not at all
- implementation recommendations may include websites, workflow changes, portals, systems, automation, or non-technical process adjustments

Why standalone discovery remains valuable:

- Blackburn Studio should deliver useful findings even if the client does not proceed with implementation
- this preserves trusted advice and avoids forcing technology before the underlying need is understood

## Workflow review activities

- current-state workflow review
- process mapping and handoff mapping
- bottleneck and repeated-work analysis
- data-entry and duplication review
- paper-form and manual-record review
- spreadsheet and email workflow review
- self-service opportunity identification
- risk and dependency identification

## Process simplification principles

- Remove unnecessary steps before introducing new systems.
- Clarify ownership and decision points.
- Standardise recurring tasks where useful.
- Prefer practical, proportionate improvements over large redesigns.
- Validate proposed changes against day-to-day operational reality.

## Paper-to-digital considerations

- Identify which paper workflows should remain manual and which should be digitised.
- Preserve necessary governance, approvals, and traceability when moving from paper to digital.
- Avoid replicating paper complexity in a digital interface without simplification.
- Sequence paper-to-digital shifts in stages to reduce operational disruption.

## When automation is appropriate

- Repetitive tasks are clearly defined and stable.
- Inputs and decision logic are understood.
- The process has been simplified enough to automate responsibly.
- The team can support the automated flow operationally.

## When a portal or custom system is appropriate

- Role-based access, tracking, or structured self-service is needed.
- Existing tools cannot support the required workflow reliability.
- Workflow complexity and scale justify a dedicated implementation.
- Discovery confirms ongoing operational value from a portal or system.

## When a simple process change is enough

- Friction is caused mainly by unclear ownership, sequencing, or communication.
- Existing tools can support the improved process with minimal changes.
- A short policy or workflow adjustment resolves the primary bottleneck.
- A staged approach can deliver value before any larger build.

## Possible deliverables

- workflow review summary
- current-state process map
- friction and bottleneck register
- prioritised improvement recommendations
- paper-to-digital process design
- simplified forms and approvals design
- implementation roadmap
- proof of concept
- staged implementation plan
- implemented workflow improvements

## Boundaries and exclusions

- Not generic management consulting.
- Not large enterprise transformation programmes.
- Not technology implementation without first understanding the process.
- Not automating a poor process without considering simplification first.
- Not unsupported financial or productivity claims.

## Evidence needed

- examples showing workflow clarity gains or reduced duplication
- evidence standards for any published operational outcome claims
- documented permission to publish client outcomes or testimonials
- repeatable discovery structure and scoped deliverables

## Relationship to Websites

- Websites remain the simplest public entry point for Digital.
- Workflow and process improvement can run before, during, or after website work where operational friction is the real issue.
- Website recommendations should reflect workflow findings rather than assumptions.

## Relationship to Platforms and portals

- Platforms and portals are implementation options when discovery confirms they are warranted.
- They are not default starting assumptions.
- Scope should remain proportionate to operational need.

## Relationship to Ongoing digital improvement

- Ongoing digital improvement can continue staged optimisation after initial changes.
- Discovery outputs can become a practical backlog for phased delivery.
- Follow-on work should remain evidence-led and operationally realistic.
