# Evidence and Case-Study Framework

Status: Proposed

Last reviewed: 2026-06-28

## Purpose

Define how projects are assessed before they are used as public proof of Blackburn Studio Digital capability.

## Candidate project categories

- Websites
- Workflow and process improvement
- Paper-to-digital modernisation
- Platforms and portals
- Business systems
- Automation
- Ongoing digital improvement
- Combined Photography and Digital work

## Assessment criteria

For each candidate project, assess:

- relevance to the desired Digital offer
- client or project type
- original request
- underlying problem
- Blackburn Studio contribution
- whether the work was paid, internal, volunteer, or personal
- ownership of the work
- permission to identify the client
- permission to show screenshots or data
- whether anonymisation is required
- evidence available
- measurable outcomes available
- accuracy of any claimed result
- confidentiality risk
- technical relevance
- visual quality
- ability to explain the work simply
- whether the project attracts the kind of future work desired

## Evidence strength levels

### Level 1: Demonstrated capability

Definition:

- Demonstrates Blackburn Studio can deliver relevant work of this type.

Safe claim range:

- capability and approach can be described
- no outcome claims beyond what is directly shown

Minimum evidence:

- verifiable artifact of work and clear attribution of contribution

### Level 2: Supported qualitative outcome

Definition:

- Includes credible qualitative evidence about improvement, usability, clarity, or process quality.

Safe claim range:

- qualitative outcomes can be described with source context
- avoid quantified impact unless evidence supports it

Minimum evidence:

- client/stakeholder feedback, observation notes, or documented before/after qualitative comparison with permission

### Level 3: Supported measurable outcome

Definition:

- Includes evidence-backed quantitative result that can be attributed proportionately.

Safe claim range:

- measured outcomes may be published where evidence and permission are confirmed

Minimum evidence:

- traceable metric source, period context, attribution boundaries, and publication permission

## Publication statuses

- Candidate
- Evidence required
- Permission required
- Approved for anonymised use
- Approved for named use
- Not suitable for publication
- Deferred

## Case-study structure

- client context
- original request
- underlying problem identified
- current-state process
- approach
- improvement recommended
- implementation
- outcome
- evidence
- client permission
- related services
- future opportunities

## Ethical and accuracy principles

- do not imply Blackburn Studio delivered work completed by others
- distinguish paid, volunteer, personal, and internal projects
- do not fabricate metrics
- do not reveal private operational information
- do not publish client identities or screenshots without permission
- make the scope of Blackburn Studio's contribution clear
- describe outcomes proportionately
