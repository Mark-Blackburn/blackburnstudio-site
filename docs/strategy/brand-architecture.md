# Brand Architecture

Status: Provisional

Last reviewed: 2026-06-28

## Purpose

This document sets out a provisional brand-architecture model for expanding Blackburn Studio beyond photography while protecting the identity described in [../current-state/brand-baseline.md](../current-state/brand-baseline.md) and [../current-state/site-overview.md](../current-state/site-overview.md).

## Proposed umbrella structure

Approved working direction:

- Blackburn Studio
- Photography
- Digital

In this model, Blackburn Studio remains the umbrella practice and customer-facing studio identity.

Photography and Digital are presented as distinct disciplines within that practice.

## Blackburn Studio as the umbrella practice

Approved working direction:

- Keep Blackburn Studio as the top-level identity.
- Use Photography and Digital as discipline labels, navigation labels, and service-group labels.
- Avoid creating a separate confirmed public brand such as Blackburn Studio Digital unless a later owner decision changes this.

Reasoning:

- The current site already establishes Blackburn Studio as the public brand.
- The supplied direction proposes a broader creative and digital practice rather than a separate business model.
- The studio name is broad enough to contain both disciplines without discarding existing photography equity.

## Photography and Digital as distinct disciplines

Approved working direction:

- Present Photography and Digital as parallel disciplines under the same studio.
- Do not describe Photography as a supporting add-on to Digital.
- Do not describe Digital as a side note beneath Photography.

This is consistent with the supplied direction that Photography must remain complete and visible.

## Why Photography should remain prominent

Photography should remain prominent because:

- it is the current public identity of the website, as documented in [../current-state/site-overview.md](../current-state/site-overview.md)
- it carries the strongest current brand distinctiveness, as documented in [../current-state/brand-baseline.md](../current-state/brand-baseline.md)
- it expresses the tone and sensibility that the Digital discipline is expected to inherit rather than replace
- reducing it to a supporting service would contradict the supplied strategic direction

## Why Digital fits within the Blackburn Studio name

Approved working direction rationale:

- Blackburn Studio already reads as a practice name rather than a narrow product label.
- The word studio can credibly encompass thoughtful digital work if the presentation remains personal, restrained, and capability-led rather than agency-led.
- The proposed Digital scope includes workflow and process improvement, paper-to-digital modernisation, and proportionate implementation in stages where appropriate.
- This remains compatible with a considered practice model rather than requiring a large-firm identity.

## Relationship between the two disciplines

Approved working direction:

- Photography and Digital share a parent brand, tone, and standard of craft.
- They remain operationally distinct enough that a client can engage one without needing the other.
- They may overlap when a client needs both imagery and digital implementation.

Shared brand traits expected across both disciplines:

- human and calm tone
- low-hype language
- quiet confidence
- restrained visual design
- thoughtful curation
- focus on real people and practical outcomes

Shared working discipline across Photography and Digital:

- observation
- understanding people and context
- thoughtful composition
- removing unnecessary noise
- finding a clearer way to present or perform the work

This is not a claim that photography and software are the same craft. It is a practical statement that both disciplines benefit from careful observation, problem framing, and clear execution.

## How combined Photography and Digital engagements could work

Approved working direction examples:

- a small business website project that also needs original photography
- a professional portfolio where site design and imagery are developed together
- a community organisation that needs both member-facing content and usable digital structure

Important clarification:

- combined engagements should be presented as useful where relevant, not as mandatory bundling

## How the practice avoids appearing fragmented

Approved working direction:

- keep a single umbrella brand and consistent verbal tone
- use a shared visual system across both disciplines
- make discipline boundaries clear in navigation and page structure
- explain Digital in the same calm, practical voice already present in Photography content
- avoid introducing multiple competing sub-brands

## How the practice avoids presenting itself as a generic full-service agency

Approved working direction:

- define a narrow enough Digital scope to remain credible
- foreground practical outcomes and considered delivery rather than large-agency breadth
- avoid listing every possible creative, marketing, or IT service
- present Mark Blackburn as the lead practitioner rather than implying a large delivery team

## How the personal reputation of Mark Blackburn supports the studio

Approved working direction states that Blackburn Studio is a personal, experienced, and considered practice led by Mark Blackburn.

Approved working direction implication:

- Mark Blackburn's reputation can support trust, continuity, and credibility across both Photography and Digital.
- His role should strengthen the sense of a led practice, not turn the studio into a generic consultancy profile page.

Requires owner review:

- how explicitly his engineering background should be surfaced in public-facing copy
- how much of the brand should lean on his personal identity versus the studio identity

## Naming conventions for navigation, page headings, and service references

Approved working direction naming guidance:

- top-level umbrella: Blackburn Studio
- discipline labels: Photography, Digital
- photography references: Portraits, Families, Couples, Personal work
- digital references: Websites, Platforms and portals, Business systems and automation, Ongoing digital support

Recommended usage:

- use Photography and Digital as clear discipline labels in navigation
- use service-group names as page headings or section headings under Digital
- avoid presenting Blackburn Studio Digital as a separate confirmed legal or public brand

## What this model does not mean

This proposed structure does not mean:

- every photography client needs Digital services
- every Digital client needs photography
- photography becomes subordinate to web development
- Blackburn Studio claims to provide every possible creative or IT service
- the practice operates like a large agency

## Unresolved questions requiring owner review

- What exact public biography wording best expresses Mark Blackburn's engineering background without shifting into consultancy-style language?
- After launch evidence is gathered, are additional Digital routes needed beyond `/digital`?

## Strategic assumptions introduced here

- Blackburn Studio can credibly remain the umbrella identity for both disciplines.
- The current photography tone can carry into Digital without becoming incoherent.
- The studio should present as a led practice rather than a scaled agency.
