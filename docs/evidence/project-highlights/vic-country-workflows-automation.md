## Role in Blackburn Studio evidence

This project is the strongest current evidence for workflow and process improvement, paper-to-digital modernisation, business systems, automation, and ongoing digital improvement in Blackburn Studio Digital.

It demonstrates how Mark Blackburn identified broader operational weaknesses, then designed and delivered a more structured Microsoft 365 and automation-based operating model for a volunteer-run organisation.

## Target audience relevance

- sporting organisations
- member organisations
- volunteer-run organisations
- organisations with fragmented administration
- organisations needing Microsoft 365 workflow modernisation
- organisations needing governance and compliance visibility
- organisations needing repeatable reporting and handover processes
- organisations moving from spreadsheets, paper forms, and inbox-based administration

## Client or organisational situation

- original request or starting point:
  - there was no single client request defining the full workflow program
  - the work emerged during the broader Microsoft 365 and infrastructure engagement
- underlying problem:
  - information was distributed across personal inboxes
  - Google Workspace and personal Google accounts were in use
  - compliance tracking was spreadsheet-based or inconsistent
  - paper forms, repeated data entry, and manual reminders were common
  - board visibility and operational continuity were limited
- additional opportunities identified by Mark Blackburn:
  - Microsoft 365 migration
  - role-based league and club accounts
  - board and executive collaboration structures
  - central governance and compliance document management
  - structured club information
  - form-based data collection
  - compliance tracking
  - automated reminders
  - reporting
  - per-club summaries
  - board-level visibility
  - PDF reporting
  - automated email distribution
  - sentiment analysis
  - issue flagging
  - due-date tracking
  - easier volunteer handover
  - broader process standardisation

This program should be described as an identified and initiated modernisation effort, not as a single pre-defined client brief.
